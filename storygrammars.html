Narrative Comprehension

Story Grammars

What processes do you use to build a story you hear or read? The answer is inference processes, particularly bridging inferences which connect this part of the story with what came earlier in it. What does the brain use to do this? My lab is helping put together a study to look at this. We are going to be using a variety of techniques to quantify the hypothesized likelihood that a bridgning inference will be made. To do this, we are relying on both causal story grammars (Trebasso & Sperry, 1985) and other techniques.
